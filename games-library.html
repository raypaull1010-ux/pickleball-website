2-Person Games

7 games

Dink Wars

Easy

Battle of consistency. First to 11 points wins - you score when your opponent pops up.

How to Play:

  1. Both players at the kitchen line
  2. Dink cross-court only (or straight - your choice)
  3. Score a point when opponent hits attackable pop-up
  4. Lose a point for errors (net/out)
  5. First to 11, win by 2

What We Track:

  • Pop-up count per player
  • Rally lengths
  • Winner declaration
  • Consistency streaks

Target Practice

Medium

Place targets (cones, towels) on the court. Hit them for points. Most points wins.

How to Play:

  1. Set up 3-5 targets in opponent's court
  2. Take turns hitting serves/returns/dinks
  3. 1 point for hitting target zone
  4. Bonus points for direct hits
  5. 20 shots each, highest score wins

What We Track:

  • Target accuracy %
  • Zone hit heatmap
  • Score breakdown
  • FH vs BH accuracy

King of the Kitchen

Easy

Stay in the rally as long as possible. Loser gets a point against them.

How to Play:

  1. Both players dink at the kitchen
  2. Rally until someone makes an error
  3. Error = 1 point against you
  4. First to 7 points loses
  5. Optional: allow speed-ups

What We Track:

  • Points against each player
  • Average rally length
  • Error types
  • Longest rally achieved

Speed-up Roulette

Hard

Dink until someone speeds up. The other player must reset. Tests attack & defense.

How to Play:

  1. Dink cross-court normally
  2. Either player can speed up anytime
  3. Defender must reset to kitchen
  4. Point for successful reset OR winner
  5. First to 11 points

What We Track:

  • Speed-up success rate
  • Reset success rate
  • Reaction time
  • Point outcomes

21 Drops

Medium

Alternate third shot drops. Race to 21 "perfect" drops that land in the kitchen.

How to Play:

  1. One player feeds, one drops
  2. Perfect drop (kitchen) = 1 point
  3. Transition zone = 0 points
  4. Deep/net/out = -1 point
  5. First to 21 wins, then switch roles

What We Track:

  • Perfect drops count
  • Landing zone breakdown
  • Consistency %
  • Final scores

Crossfire

Medium

Cross-court dinking only. No straight shots allowed. Tests angles and footwork.

How to Play:

  1. Both start on same side (both left or both right)
  2. Only cross-court shots allowed
  3. Straight shot = lose point
  4. Error = lose point
  5. First to 11 wins

What We Track:

  • Cross-court accuracy
  • Illegal straight shots
  • Rally consistency
  • Angle variety

Serve & Return Battle

Easy

Points for deep serves AND deep returns. Both players can score every rally.

How to Play:

  1. Alternate serving
  2. Deep serve (back 1/3) = 1 point for server
  3. Deep return (past kitchen) = 1 point for returner
  4. Both can score same rally
  5. 20 serves each, highest total wins

What We Track:

  • Serve depth %
  • Return depth %
  • Points per player
  • Depth consistency

Rotating King

Medium

2v1 format. Winner stays, loser rotates out. Track individual win rates.

How to Play:

  1. Two players vs one "King"
  2. Play points rally-style
  3. If King wins point, they stay
  4. If challengers win, King rotates out
  5. Track total wins per player

What We Track:

  • Individual win %
  • Longest King streak
  • Points played per player
  • Shot quality stats

2v1 Reset Challenge

Hard

Two attackers vs one defender. Defender tries to reset. Great pressure practice.

How to Play:

  1. 2 players at kitchen attack
  2. 1 player in transition defends
  3. Defender resets to kitchen = 1 point
  4. Attackers win point = 1 point for them
  5. Play to 7, rotate defender

What We Track:

  • Reset success % per player
  • Attack success %
  • Rally survival rate
  • Pressure handling rating

Rotating Thirds

Medium

One feeds, one drops, one evaluates. Rotate roles. Compare everyone's drops.

How to Play:

  1. Player A feeds from baseline
  2. Player B hits third shot drop
  3. Player C catches at kitchen, evaluates
  4. 10 drops each, then rotate
  5. Best average score wins

What We Track:

  • Per-player drop stats
  • Landing zone breakdown
  • Consistency comparison
  • Winner determination

4-Person Games

6 games

Skinny Singles

Medium

Half-court 1v1 games. Two matches happening simultaneously. Full individual tracking.

How to Play:

  1. Split court in half (down the middle)
  2. 2 separate 1v1 games on each half
  3. Standard scoring to 11
  4. Winner rotates to play other winner
  5. Track all 4 players individually

What We Track:

  • Full stats per player
  • Win/loss records
  • Head-to-head matchups
  • Comparative rankings

Round Robin Dinks

Easy

4 players rotate positions. Track consistency across all kitchen positions.

How to Play:

  1. 4 players at kitchen (2 per side)
  2. Dink in pattern (diagonal or around)
  3. Error = point against you
  4. Rotate positions every 2 minutes
  5. Most consistent player wins

What We Track:

  • Per-player consistency
  • Stats by position
  • Pop-up rates
  • Overall rankings

Firefight 4-Way

Hard

2v2 speed-up battles. Dink until someone attacks, then it's firefight time.

How to Play:

  1. Standard 2v2 at kitchen
  2. Dink until someone speeds up
  3. Point ends when someone wins/errors
  4. First team to 11 wins
  5. Track team AND individual stats

What We Track:

  • Team scores
  • Individual hand speed ratings
  • Speed-up success rates
  • Firefight win %

Poaching Drill

Medium

Practice switching and poaching. Points for successful poaches.

How to Play:

  1. Standard 2v2 dinking
  2. Either team can poach anytime
  3. Successful poach = 2 points
  4. Failed poach = 1 point to opponent
  5. First team to 15 wins

What We Track:

  • Poach attempt rate
  • Poach success %
  • Communication rating
  • Team coordination score

Rotate & Attack

Medium

One team attacks, one defends. Rotate roles. Compare attack vs defense stats.

How to Play:

  1. Team A attacks from kitchen
  2. Team B defends from transition
  3. Point for winner or successful reset
  4. Switch roles every 10 points
  5. Best combined score wins

What We Track:

  • Attack success per player
  • Reset success per player
  • Role effectiveness comparison
  • Team balance analysis

Kitchen Chaos

Hard

4-way dink rally with random speed-ups allowed. Total chaos, maximum fun.

How to Play:

  1. 4 players at kitchen, 2v2
  2. Anyone can dink OR attack anytime
  3. No pattern required
  4. Point for every error opponent makes
  5. First team to 15 wins

What We Track:

  • Reaction times
  • Dink vs attack ratio
  • Consistency under pressure
  • Chaos survival rating
