Serve Drills

Master your serve with placement, speed, and consistency tracking

Beginner

Serve Depth Drill

Practice hitting serves deep into the service box. Focus on consistent depth rather than speed.

What We Track:

  • Depth (short/medium/deep zones)
  • Consistency % to target zone
  • Error rate (net/out)
  • FH vs BH serve comparison
Tip: Aim for the back 1/3 of the service box. Deep serves give your partner less angle.
Intermediate

Serve Placement Drill

Target specific zones - corners, body, and T-serves. Develop placement variety.

What We Track:

  • Placement heatmap (9 zones)
  • Target accuracy %
  • Serve speed (mph)
  • Spin type detection
Tip: Mix up your targets. Don't hit the same spot twice in a row.
Advanced

Power Serve Drill

Maximize serve speed while maintaining accuracy. Push your limits.

What We Track:

  • Speed tracking (mph)
  • Speed vs accuracy correlation
  • Fault rate at max speed
  • Optimal speed zone identification
Tip: Find your "80% speed" - fast enough to pressure, consistent enough to trust.

Return Drills

Deep, consistent returns that neutralize the serve

Beginner

Deep Return Drill

Every return should land past the kitchen line. Focus on depth over power.

What We Track:

  • Landing depth (feet from baseline)
  • Deep return % (past kitchen)
  • FH vs BH depth comparison
  • Error breakdown
Tip: Aim 2-3 feet inside the baseline. Margin is your friend on returns.
Intermediate

Return & Rush Drill

Hit deep return and immediately move forward. Track shot quality while transitioning.

What We Track:

  • Return depth
  • Speed of return
  • Court position after return
  • Split-step timing
Tip: Don't sacrifice depth for speed. A deep return buys you time to move up.
Advanced

Targeted Return Drill

Hit returns to specific targets - backhand corner, body, or down the line.

What We Track:

  • Target accuracy %
  • Placement heatmap
  • Speed by target zone
  • Winner/error ratio
Tip: Most players' backhand is weaker. Attack it early and often.

Third Shot Drills

Drops, drives, and the most important shot in pickleball

Beginner

Third Shot Drop - Basics

Get the ball into the kitchen with a soft, arcing drop shot.

What We Track:

  • Landing zone (kitchen/transition/deep)
  • Arc height (high/medium/low-perfect)
  • Consistency %
  • Net clearance
Tip: The apex of your drop should be on YOUR side of the net, then fall into the kitchen.
Intermediate

Third Shot Drive

Attack with a low, fast drive when opponent gives you a high ball.

What We Track:

  • Drive speed (mph)
  • Trajectory (high/ok/low-perfect)
  • Spin movement detection
  • Placement accuracy
Tip: Aim at the feet or the backhand hip. Make them hit up.
Advanced

Drop vs Drive Decision

Practice reading the situation and choosing the right third shot.

What We Track:

  • Drive vs drop ratio
  • Success rate by choice
  • Decision quality rating
  • Pattern recognition
Tip: Drive when they're back or give you a high ball. Drop when they're at the kitchen.

Volley Drills

Punch volleys, drive volleys, and net play

Beginner

Punch Volley Basics

Short, compact volleys with minimal backswing. Keep it simple.

What We Track:

  • Pop-up % (attackable returns)
  • Low/unattackable %
  • Placement accuracy
  • Error rate (net/out)
Tip: Punch, don't swing. Think "catch and redirect" the ball.
Intermediate

Drive Volley Drill

Attack high balls with pace while staying balanced.

What We Track:

  • Speed tracking
  • Winner vs error ratio
  • Trajectory analysis
  • Recovery position
Tip: Step INTO the volley. Weight moving forward = more power, better control.
Advanced

Reflex Volley Drill

Fast-hands practice for rapid exchanges at the net.

What We Track:

  • Reaction time
  • Rally length sustained
  • Error under pressure %
  • Hand speed rating
Tip: Soften your grip. A looser grip = faster reactions and better touch.

Reset & Counter Drills

Defend attacks and neutralize with soft hands

Beginner

Basic Reset Drill

Absorb pace and drop the ball softly into the kitchen.

What We Track:

  • Reset success %
  • Landing zone accuracy
  • Soft hands rating
  • Ball height over net
Tip: Let the ball come to you. Absorb with a loose grip and open paddle face.
Intermediate

Block & Reset Combo

Block the first attack, reset the second. Two-shot defense sequence.

What We Track:

  • Block success rate
  • Reset after block %
  • Sequence completion rate
  • Recovery position
Tip: First block just needs to stay low. Second shot resets to the kitchen.
Advanced

Counter-Attack Drill

Turn defense into offense. Reset, then attack when they pop up.

What We Track:

  • Counter-attack success %
  • Pattern recognition
  • Transition from defense to offense
  • Point win rate after reset
Tip: Be patient. Reset until THEY pop up, then attack.

Dink Drills

The foundation of high-level pickleball

Beginner

Straight-Ahead Dinks

Basic dinking drill - keep it in the kitchen, straight to your partner.

What We Track:

  • Pop-up rate %
  • Consistency (rally length)
  • Net clearance
  • FH vs BH breakdown
Tip: Lift with your legs, not your wrist. Think "push" not "hit."
Intermediate

Cross-Court Dinks

Move the ball diagonally - more angle, more margin over the net.

What We Track:

  • Cross-court L‚ÜíR accuracy
  • Cross-court R‚ÜíL accuracy
  • Angle consistency
  • Pop-up rate by direction
Tip: Cross-court dinks have 3 feet more margin. Use it to your advantage.
Advanced

Attacking Dink Patterns

Set up attacks with strategic dink placement. Move them, then strike.

What We Track:

  • Attack dink %
  • Dead dink % (neutral)
  • Pattern sequences
  • Points won after attack dink
Tip: Move them side to side, then attack the open court or their backhand.

Overhead Drills

Put away lobs and high balls with authority

Beginner

Basic Overhead Drill

Focus on contact point and getting the ball in play.

What We Track:

  • In-play rate %
  • Error breakdown (net/out)
  • Placement zones
  • Footwork rating
Tip: Turn sideways, point at the ball with your non-paddle hand, then swing.
Intermediate

Placement Overhead

Target specific zones - corners, body, or back at their feet.

What We Track:

  • Target accuracy %
  • Put-away rate
  • Speed by placement
  • Winner % by zone
Tip: Don't always crush it. A well-placed overhead at 70% is often more effective.
Advanced

Moving Overhead Drill

Handle lobs that push you back. Backpedal, set, execute.

What We Track:

  • Recovery after overhead
  • Success rate while moving
  • Decision quality (hit vs let bounce)
  • Balance rating
Tip: If you're struggling, let it bounce. A controlled bounce smash beats a wild overhead.

Transition Zone Drills

Navigate the danger zone between baseline and kitchen

Intermediate

Drop & Move Forward

Hit a drop, move forward, handle the response. Continuous progression.

What We Track:

  • Successful transitions %
  • Steps forward per drop
  • Position at opponent contact
  • Next shot quality
Tip: Move forward AFTER your drop crosses the net, not before.
Intermediate

Split-Step Timing

Practice the split-step at the perfect moment for optimal reaction.

What We Track:

  • Split-step timing accuracy
  • Balance on landing
  • First-step direction efficiency
  • Reaction to ball direction
Tip: Split-step when opponent is about to hit. Land balanced, ready to move either way.
Advanced

Transition Under Pressure

Handle attacks while moving through the transition zone.

What We Track:

  • Reset success while moving
  • Balance under attack
  • Decision quality (reset vs counter)
  • Point completion rate
Tip: Stay low through the transition. High = easy target. Low = harder to attack.

Lob Drills

Offensive weapons and defensive escapes

Intermediate

Offensive Lob

Catch them off guard with a perfectly placed lob over their reach.

What We Track:

  • Lob success rate
  • Height/depth accuracy
  • Winner % from lobs
  • Disguise rating
Tip: Use the same setup as a dink. The disguise makes the lob effective.

What We Track:

  • Depth accuracy
  • Short lobs % (punished)
  • Point continuation rate
Tip: Height is your friend on defensive lobs. High = more time to recover.

Speed-up & Firefight Drills

Fast hands and quick decisions at the kitchen line

Intermediate

Controlled Speed-up

Practice initiating speed-ups with control, not just power.

What We Track:

  • Speed-up success rate
  • Target accuracy (body/feet)

  • Error type breakdown
Tip: Stay compact. Smaller swings = faster recovery for next ball.
