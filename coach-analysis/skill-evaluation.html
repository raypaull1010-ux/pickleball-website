Skill Level Guide

Understanding pickleball skill ratings

2.5
Learning basic rules and techniques. Can sustain a short rally. Developing serve and return consistency.
3.0
Comfortable with all basic shots. Understands positioning. Can play competitively with similar-level players.
3.5
Consistent with fundamentals. Has control over power and placement. Beginning to develop strategy and shot selection.
4.0
Strong all-around game. Can vary speed, spin, and placement. Makes few unforced errors. Good court awareness.
4.5
Advanced skills and strategy. Can attack and defend effectively. Excellent shot anticipation and placement.
5.0+
Tournament-level player. Exceptional skills in all areas. Can compete at the highest amateur levels.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this an official DUPR rating? +

No, this is an independent evaluation by Ray. For official DUPR ratings, you need to play sanctioned matches that report results to DUPR. However, Ray's evaluation gives you an accurate sense of your skill level based on the same criteria used by official rating systems.

What video should I submit? +

Ideally, submit 2-5 minutes of competitive gameplay (not drilling). The video should show a variety of situations - serves, returns, dinks, drives, volleys, and point play. Film from a position where your full body and court position are visible.

How long until I get my results? +

Most evaluations are completed within 24-48 hours. You'll receive an email with your skill level and detailed evaluation as soon as Ray finishes reviewing your video.

Can I submit another video later to track progress? +

Absolutely! Many players submit videos every few months to track their improvement. It's a great way to see how your training is paying off and identify new areas to focus on.

What payment methods do you accept? +

We accept credit/debit cards through Stripe and Venmo. Both options are secure and easy to use. You'll choose your preferred payment method during checkout.

How is this different from video analysis? +

Video analysis focuses on breaking down your technique and providing detailed coaching on how to improve specific shots. Skill evaluation is specifically designed to answer "what level am I?" - giving you a rating and explaining where you stand. Many players do both!
